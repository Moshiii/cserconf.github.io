SCAM 2012 will to have a Tool Papers track and invites tool papers of six pages length. Please consider submitting a tool paper outlining the details of your tool and your experience in building such tools. The tool could be in any area under the umbrella of SCAM. Tool papers are significantly different from traditional tool demonstration papers. Some details are follows:
  1. A tool paper is neither like a demo paper, nor like a research paper.
  2. It aptly and accurately describes a tool,
  3. Motivates its existence well (and relates it to what came before),
  4. Describes its architecture and explains its inner workings, and most appealing use cases.
  5. Describes experience gained in developing the tool.
  6. Does not necessarily compare with other tools (this is not a research paper)
Note that the submission length has been expanded to a maximum of six pages, compared to the two to four pages of traditional tool demo papers. This gives authors enough space to discuss use cases and tool design in much more detail. A demo scenario with screenshots and information about tool availability can also be included but is not a requirement.

The key criterion for acceptance is that the paper should make an original contribution that can benefit others engaged in designing and building tools for source code analysis and manipulation. The tool need not be entirely ready for deployment.

Authors are encouraged to reference supporting resources, including video demos of their tool, and reviewers may take this information into account as they review the paper. However, such materials will not become part of the permanent record of the conference, so the paper should stand on its own. In order to preserve reviewer anonymity, such materials should be hosted on an anonymous public source (e.g., youtube), or made available in such a way that the tools chair can download once and redistribute to reviewers. If authors would like to inform reviewers of additional information that does not appear in the paper, such information can be emailed to the Tools Chair Chanchal Roy at croy@cs.usask.ca. Authors of the accepted tool papers will be invited to present their tool at the conference. All the accepted tool papers will be published in the conference proceedings.
